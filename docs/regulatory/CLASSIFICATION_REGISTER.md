# Classification Register

> **Not legal or regulatory advice.** This register is an internal engineering and product control. A named Regulatory Lead owns the device-classification position, and a notified body or competent authority makes the final assessment. See `docs/regulatory/OWNERSHIP.md`.

## Purpose

The intended-use boundary is this product's most important and most fragile asset. The MVP is a data-aggregation, visualization, longitudinal-change, workflow, and documentation-support tool — **not** an autonomous medical decision-maker. Commercial pull toward biological-age, "optimal" ranges, and risk scores would likely qualify the product as Medical Device Software (MDSW) under EU MDR Annex VIII, Rule 11, triggering a notified body, clinical evaluation, an ISO 13485 QMS, and post-market surveillance.

This register is the gate that keeps the MVP on the documentation-support side of that line. **Every feature that touches clinical meaning — rules, highlighting, narrative, reference ranges, scores, or comparisons — is recorded and checked here before merge.** A feature on the Prohibited MVP behavior list is BLOCKED until a documented product decision plus clinical, privacy, and regulatory reviews are complete. Claims discipline applies in parallel: marketing, sales, and onboarding copy are reviewed against the same boundary as the software, because intended purpose as stated in claims often drives classification more than the code does.

### How to use this register

1. Before merging a feature that touches clinical meaning, add or update a row.
2. Set **Touches clinical meaning?** honestly; if Yes, the Regulatory Lead must sign off before merge.
3. If the **Classification impact** is anything other than "stays within documentation-support boundary", treat the row as a gate, not a task.
4. Keep the **Status** column current: `Shipped`, `In review`, `Pending (gated)`, or `BLOCKED`.

### Status legend

- **Shipped** — implemented and within the documentation-support boundary.
- **In review** — under clinical/privacy/regulatory review before merge.
- **Pending (gated)** — depends on an external gate (vendor decision, DPA/TOMs, formal classification determination); not yet started or not yet permitted.
- **BLOCKED** — on the Prohibited MVP behavior list; not implemented; raised for the documented review process only.

- **Full patient-invitation flow** (staff invite endpoint + patient-side consent capture + real out-of-band secure delivery channel) — the mechanics are buildable on synthetic identities, but pointing any of it at a real patient is BLOCKED behind the Regulatory-Lead assignment, AVV/TOMs/§203, DPIA + ROPA, lawful basis, MDR/MDSW determination, an EU-residency delivery sub-processor, and a pen-test. Status: **Pending (gated)**.
- **Patient/tenant erasure (right-to-be-forgotten) design** — the append-only `consent_events` ledger uses `ON DELETE RESTRICT`, so a patient/tenant cannot be hard-deleted while consent events exist (the ledger must survive for legal-basis defensibility). A real retain-then-erase strategy (retention period, what is erased vs. retained, the DeletionRequest execution path across DB + derived artifacts + object storage) is a DPO/counsel deliverable, not yet built. Owner: DPO/counsel. Status: **Pending (gated)**.
